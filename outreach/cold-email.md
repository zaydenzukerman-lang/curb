# Curb — Cold Email Playbook

Send from hello@curbwebsites.com (real mailbox, SPF/DKIM set). 10–15/day week 1, max 30/day after.
Plain text. No links in Email 1. No attachments ever. Personalize the [bracketed] parts — 2 minutes each, never skip.

---

## EMAIL 1 — the linkless opener (day 0)
**Subject:** built [Company] a new website

> Hey [FirstName] —
>
> I was looking at pressure washing companies around [Town] and found [Company] — [one real specific: "300+ five-star reviews but the website's a Facebook page" / "your site doesn't load right on a phone, and that's where everyone will see it"].
>
> So this week I went ahead and built you a new one. Booking form, click-to-call, your reviews front and center.
>
> Want the link? No charge to look.
>
> — Z, Curb

*Why linkless: new domains sending links = spam folder. A reply says "send it" → now YOU were invited. Reply rate is the whole game.*

## EMAIL 2 — follow-up (day 3, only if no reply)
**Subject:** re: built [Company] a new website

> Hey [FirstName] — still have that site I built for [Company] sitting here. Takes 10 seconds to look at on your phone.
>
> If the timing's wrong or you're not interested, just tell me and I'll delete it — no hard feelings.

*The "I'll delete it" is real scarcity. One follow-up only at this stage.*

## EMAIL 3 — the breakup (day 7, only if no reply)
**Subject:** closing the file on [Company]

> [FirstName] — last note from me. I'm repurposing the [Company] site build for another [Town]-area company this week unless you want first look.
>
> Either way — good luck with the season. It's a busy one.

*Breakup emails get the highest reply rate of any email in the sequence. Then STOP. Never send a 4th.*

## WHEN THEY REPLY "send it" → the pitch message
> Here you go: [demo link]
>
> It's yours if you want it — $500 setup when you pair it with our lead gen launch (that's the part that actually makes the phone ring: Google Business optimization, review system, call tracking so you see exactly what it earns you). Or $1,200 for the site on its own.
>
> Full pricing: [pricing link]. And heads up — our first 3 clients get the 3-month launch at 40% off in exchange for an honest review. Two ways to do this, happy to walk you through it on a quick call or right here over email.

## REPLY PLAYBOOK (copy-paste answers)
**"How much?"** → "Site on its own is $1,200. Smarter move is $500 + our lead gen launch — Growth tier is normally $1,920 for 3 months, founding clients get it at $1,150. After 3 months it's month-to-month, cancel anytime."
**"Why so cheap / what's the catch?"** → "No catch — we're new to [Town] and we only work with pressure washing companies. First clients get founding pricing in exchange for a review. The site's already built; you saw it."
**"I already got a guy / my nephew does it"** → "All good. Keep the link — if the phone ever gets quiet, that's what we fix."
**"Does this really work?"** → "We track every call that comes through the site and send you the report monthly. You'll see the number. If it's not working, you cancel — after the launch it's month-to-month."
**"Who are you?"** → "Small studio, one niche — pressure washing companies. Here's our work: [apex/tidalwave links]. We keep it small on purpose: your calls get answered."
**"Can you do my Facebook?"** → "We focus where your customers actually search — Google. Ads on Facebook, yes (that's our Full Funnel tier), but we don't bill you for posting to your page. It doesn't book jobs."
**Ghosts after the link** → one nudge day 2: "Any thoughts? Honest feedback helps me even if it's a no."

## CLOSING → payment
> Awesome. Two things and we're rolling:
> 1. Invoice — [Stripe payment link] ($500 setup + $1,150 founding Growth launch = $1,650)
> 2. A quick agreement PDF (protects both of us) — reply "agreed" to accept.
> The second those clear I send you a short list of what I need (photos, your services, prices) and you're live this week.

## RULES
- Every send logged in leads.md: date, email #, status (sent/replied/pitched/closed/dead)
- Reply within 2 hours during your 4-hour window. Speed = trust.
- Never argue with a no. "All good — good luck out there" and move on.
- If they ask something not in the playbook, tell Claude — we add the answer same day.
