# Curb — Legal Checklist (researched 2026-07-09, not legal advice)

## 1. Business structure — DAD IS THE BUSINESS. Non-negotiable.
- Contracts signed by a minor are **voidable by the minor** → savvy clients could refuse to sign,
  and our own agreement could be attacked as unenforceable. Fix: **Dad is the contracting party.**
- Simplest v1: Dad operates as **sole proprietor d/b/a "Curb"** (or just his name on the agreement:
  "Curb, operated by [Dad's full name]"). Upgrade to an LLC (~$100-300, dad as organizer/member)
  once revenue is real — LLC shields family assets if a client ever sues.
- Dad's name on: Stripe, bank account, domain registrar, ad accounts, tool subscriptions.
- Zayden = the operator doing the work; money flows through dad. (Family matter how they split it.)

## 2. Cold email — CAN-SPAM (penalties up to ~$53k PER EMAIL, so we comply exactly)
B2B cold email IS legal in the US without prior consent. Required in every commercial email:
- [ ] **Valid physical postal address** in the footer — a registered P.O. box or UPS-store mailbox
  works (get one; don't use the home address). BLOCKER: set up before first send.
- [ ] Honest From name ("Z from Curb"), accurate subject lines (ours are), no deceptive headers
- [ ] A working opt-out ("reply 'no' and I won't email again" counts) — honor within 10 days, keep
  a do-not-email list forever
- Templates in outreach/cold-email.md now include the compliant footer.

## 3. Reviews & testimonials — FTC + Google rules (we fixed a violation here)
- **NEVER trade anything of value for a Google review.** Google prohibits incentivized reviews;
  FTC's fake-review rule (2024) bans buying/incentivizing consumer reviews. Our old founding offer
  ("discount for an honest Google review") violated both. FIXED: founding trade is now
  **testimonial + case-study rights only**; we ask for a Google review separately, no strings,
  after good results.
- Case studies using founding clients: disclose "founding client received discounted pricing."
- Client-side review generation (what we sell): asking customers for reviews is fine; PAYING for
  them is not. Our review-request templates ask, never compensate. Never gate ("only ask happy
  customers to post") — Google prohibits review-gating too.

## 4. Client work claims
- Never guarantee rankings, lead counts, or revenue (already our rule — it's also the legal-safe rule).
- Report real numbers only. Call recordings: CallRail handles consent notices — enable the
  "this call may be recorded" whisper in two-party-consent states.

## 5. Money & taxes (dad + accountant, not urgent week 1)
- EIN (free, 10 min, irs.gov) once there's revenue — avoids using SSN on W-9s.
- Some states tax web-design/digital services (e.g., TX, OH taxe some of it). When invoicing
  clients in those states, dad asks his tax person. Keep every invoice + expense in one folder.
- Income is taxable regardless of age. Keep it clean from dollar one.
